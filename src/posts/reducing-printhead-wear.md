Printheads are consumable parts, but avoidable wear still costs time and money. The wrong ribbon, too much heat, contamination, poor pressure settings, and inconsistent cleaning can all shorten a head's working life.

Start with the basics: use the right ribbon for the substrate, clean the printhead with suitable wipes or swabs, and avoid increasing heat settings to compensate for a ribbon that is not suited to the application.

When print quality drops, note the printer model, ribbon grade, substrate, speed, and symptoms before ordering a replacement. That information helps separate a failed head from a setup or consumable issue.
